Why it exists

Most finance apps work against you.

They ask for your bank login, upload your transactions to their servers, and charge you monthly for the privilege. If you cancel, your data disappears. Compass was built as the alternative.

Privacy by default

No bank login. No credentials. No cloud upload. Your transaction data never leaves your machine. There is no server to receive it, no account to breach, no subscription to cancel.

Instant clarity

Drop in a CSV. Categories apply automatically. See your top spending, monthly trends, budget progress, and ghost subscriptions in seconds, with no setup required.

Full control

Your database. Your encryption key. Export everything as CSV at any time. No lock-in, no account deletion risk. The data is a file on your machine, yours to keep.

How it works

Three steps to get started.

01

Export from your bank

Log into your bank's website and go to Transactions, then Download or Export, then CSV. Every bank supports this with no special access, no new account, and no permissions to grant.

Works with Chase, Capital One, Wells Fargo, Bank of America, Navy Federal, Discover, Amex, Venmo, Cash App, PayPal, and more.
02

Drop it into Compass

The guided 6-step wizard detects your bank's column layout automatically. Bank presets pre-fill the mapping. Returning users skip straight to preview with one click.

03

See where your money went

100+ built-in categorization rules apply instantly. Navigate your Dashboard, Trends, Reports, Budgets, and Goals. Everything populates automatically with no manual entry.

Try Demo Mode first. It loads about 50 realistic sample transactions instantly with no import needed.

Features

Everything you need. Nothing you don't.

6-Step Import Wizard

Guided import with column auto-detection. Bank presets for Chase, Capital One, Wells Fargo, Discover, Amex, Venmo, and more. Batch import multiple files. Duplicate detection via content hash.

Auto-Categorization

100+ built-in rules cover Amazon, Walmart, DoorDash, Starbucks, Netflix, and more. Manual corrections optionally save as rules so future imports self-categorize. Manage all rules from the sidebar.

Dashboard & Trends

Monthly income, expenses, and net savings at a glance. 3, 6, or 12-month trend charts showing income vs. expenses and per-category breakdowns. Navigate any month freely, past or future.

Budgets & Goals

Soft monthly spending limits with on-pace projections so you can catch a potential overspend before month end. Track net savings, spending limits, and income targets as goals with progress bars.

Ghost Subscription Detector

Finds recurring charges with the exact same description and amount appearing in 2 or more months. Lists monthly and estimated annual totals for every subscription you may have forgotten about.

Insights & Privacy

Automatic spending analysis surfaces budget warnings, top merchants, detected subscriptions, paycheck timing, and overdraft alerts, all processed locally. SQLCipher AES-256 encrypted database. Zero telemetry.

Security & Privacy

Questions you should ask before trusting any finance app.

Where is my data stored?
In a local SQLite database at %APPDATA%\com.compass.app\ on your Windows machine. Only your user account can access it. No other location, no remote copy.
Is the database encrypted?
Yes. SQLCipher AES-256 encrypts the database at rest. The key is a 32-byte random value stored in Windows Credential Manager (DPAPI-backed), bound to your Windows user account. It is never visible in the app UI.
What data leaves my device?
Nothing financial. The only outbound request is an optional update check against the GitHub Releases API (version numbers only, no personal data). The app works fully offline after installation.
Does Compass collect telemetry?
No. There is no telemetry SDK, analytics library, or error reporting service in the codebase. Zero background network calls beyond the optional update check.
Can the developer see my data?
No. There is no server. No sync. No account. No mechanism by which anyone other than you can access data stored on your own machine. The developer has zero visibility into your financial data, ever.
